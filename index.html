Cosmic CSS Effects

Space-inspired shape-outside, masking, blend modes, and filters

Shape Outside Effect

The shape-outside CSS property defines a shape around which adjacent inline content should wrap, allowing text to flow around cosmic objects and stellar formations.

Neptune, the farthest planet from the sun, is an ice giant with a distinctive blue color due to methane, and it's known for its strong winds and 14 moons, including the potentially captured dwarf planet, Triton.

Dark, cold, and whipped by supersonic winds, ice giant Neptune is more than 30 times as far from the Sun as Earth. Neptune is the only planet in our solar system not visible to the naked eye. In 2011 Neptune completed its first 165-year orbit since its discovery in 1846. Neptune is so far from the Sun that high noon on the big blue planet would seem like dim twilight to us. The warm light we see here on our home planet is roughly 900 times as bright as sunlight on Neptune.

The Cosmic Convergence of CSS Effects

This celestial body demonstrates all four CSS special effects working in perfect cosmic harmony. As you can clearly see, the text is flowing gracefully around the circular shape of this cosmic body - a perfect demonstration of the shape-outside property in action. Just as planets orbit stars and galaxies swirl around black holes, the text forms a natural path around this central cosmic element.

Observe how the text wraps perfectly around the circular boundary, maintaining an exact distance from its edge. This is the shape-outside effect at work, directing the flow of text content to follow the contours of the specified shape rather than the default rectangular box model.

In addition to the shape-outside property, this celestial body incorporates:

  • Masking: A radial gradient mask creates a stellar corona effect where the edges fade into the cosmic void
  • Blend Mode: The luminosity blend mode creates an otherworldly interaction with the background space
  • Filters: Hue-rotation, contrast, and saturation adjustments transform the colors into a pulsating cosmic entity

The combination of these effects creates a unique interstellar phenomenon.
